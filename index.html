Day 1: South Lake Tahoe

Rubicon Trail

In 2018, my Husband and I did an amazing hiking trip to South Lake Tahoe and Yosemite National Park. Our first day, we hit the road and explored the Rubicon Trail. This is a beautiful hike with amazing views of Lake Tahoe and it's aqua colored waters. The only thing that would have made it perfect was being out on one of those boats.

Day 2: South Lake Tahoe

Mount Tallac

The Rubicon Trail was a warm-up hike, probably about 4 miles. On this day, we decided to conquer Mount Tallac. Ten miles, total elevation just under 10,000 feet. I'll never forget this hike because we seriously underestimated the amount of water we needed. Six hours later (and a couple of wrong turns!) we finally finished.

Day 3: Yosemite National Park

Hike to Glacier Point

To date, Yosemite National Park is my favorite place I've ever hiked. At every turn, it seems like the next view is more specatcular than the last. On this day, although we were still recovering from the trip up Mount Tallac, we decided that we couldn't miss out on the opportunity to hike from the Valley to Glacier Point. Talk about straight incline. The view was the reward though!

Day 4: Hike to Cathedral Lakes

Our Final Hike at Yosemite National Park

On our final day, we explored the less visited area near the North entrance of Yosemite. One thing I won't live down about this trip is that in planning our visit, I didn't realize that the majority of the sites in Yosemite are near the Valley. I selected a hotel that was 30 minutes from the North entrance of the park, not realizing that was actually 2.5 hours from the Valley! On our last day, we wanted to spend as much time seeing the sights as possible, so we looked for a hike near the north entrance. Enter Cathedral Lakes, in all of it's glory. This was one of those hikes where you kept going deeper into the forest, not exactly sure what you would find. Then BAM. All of the sudden, the forest opened up to this massive lake. It was breathtaking.
